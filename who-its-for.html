Who it's for

Built for one shape of business.

I run the marketing for considered, high-ticket purchases — the businesses that get chosen after the customer does their research. That's where strategy, content, search, and nurture actually compound. It's the wrong tool for an impulse buy.

The short version

I work with established, profitable companies — typically $3–12M — who've outgrown referral-and-marketplace luck and want it handled, not added to their plate. I'm not a great fit for pre-revenue startups, owners shopping for the cheapest freelancer, or anyone who wants a vendor to manage rather than a leader to hand it to.

The honest fit check

Right tool, wrong tool.

A fit if…

  • Your customer researches for days or weeks before they buy — a considered, high-ticket purchase.
  • Real spend already flows across channels, and it's profitable.
  • No senior marketing leader and no agency is competently running it.
  • You can absorb more demand without breaking on lead times or quality.
  • You want to hand over the whole space, not micromanage a vendor.

Not a fit if…

  • The purchase is impulse or break-fix, with no consideration cycle to win.
  • There's no budget flowing yet — this conducts what exists, it isn't zero-to-one.
  • You're already led well, with no real gap.
  • You want a body to "just post for us," not a function run for you.
  • You want guaranteed revenue or performance-only pay.
