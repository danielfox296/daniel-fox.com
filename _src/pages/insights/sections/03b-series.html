A series

After the first product-market fit.

A four-part series on what happens when the segment that built your business runs out of people: how to read the signal, find the next cohort, and reopen the customer question you marked as settled years ago.

1. Product-market fit has a ceiling

The fit that got you to your first few million covers one segment, one channel, one story. Why it's a gradient, not a finish line.

2. Your dashboard can't tell you what's wrong

Declining return on ad spend has three different causes and one identical-looking graph. Where the real diagnosis happens.

3. The small tweak that opens the next market

When growth stalls, founders reach for a rebrand. Usually the next cohort needs a reframe, not a new story.

4. Your answers are working as designed

Your answer to “who is our customer” is calibrated to produce exactly your current numbers. How to reopen the question.
