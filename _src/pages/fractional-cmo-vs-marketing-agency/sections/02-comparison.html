The honest comparison

Four options. The real question is who owns the outcome.

Every option below can produce work. The difference isn't competence at the task — it's whether anyone is accountable for the result the task is supposed to serve, and how much of the thinking still lands back on your desk.

A marketing agency

Owns the execution of channels you point them at — ads, social, sometimes a site. They run tasks well. What stays on your desk: the strategy, the priorities, and the call on whether any of it is the right thing to be doing at all.

A freelancer

Owns one craft — the copy, the ads, the design. Often genuinely good at it. What stays on your desk: everything around that craft, plus the job of managing them and stitching their output into a plan that doesn't exist yet.

An in-house hire

Owns whatever their seniority allows. A coordinator owns coordination; a director owns direction, if you can afford a real one and attract them. What stays on your desk: the gap between the title you could fund and the leadership you actually need.

A fractional marketing leader

Owns the whole plan and every layer that serves it — strategy, search, paid, capture, nurture, measurement — for a fraction of a full-time executive's cost. What stays on your desk: the decision to say yes, and the budget. One owner, one number, one throat to choke.
