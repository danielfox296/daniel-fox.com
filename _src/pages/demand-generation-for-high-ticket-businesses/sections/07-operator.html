The operator behind the argument

This comes from someone who has run the whole thing.

I'm Daniel Fox. I founded, bootstrapped, built, and sold Skreened, a custom-apparel company that grew past 100 people and roughly $16M in revenue before it was acquired.

That matters here for one reason. The case for leading the demand function isn't a marketing theory. It's what running a high-ticket, custom-built business actually teaches you. When you've carried the P&L, scaled the team, and answered for the number yourself, you stop believing that more activity is the answer and you start seeing that the missing thing is almost always leadership. That's what I'm here to be: the strategic leader the business has never actually had, an operator who has held that seat before.
